# Governed Metric Definition: Net Interest Margin (NIM)
## Apex Bank — Official KPI Reference
### Version: 2.0 | Effective Date: 2024-01-01 | Owner: Group Finance

---

## 1. Definition

Net Interest Margin (NIM) measures the difference between interest income
generated by the bank's earning assets and the interest paid on its
liabilities (deposits and borrowings), expressed as a percentage of average
earning assets. NIM is the primary indicator of the bank's core lending
profitability and pricing effectiveness.

---

## 2. Official Formula

```
NIM (%) = (Total Interest Income - Total Interest Expense)
          ─────────────────────────────────────────────────  × 100
                    Average Earning Assets
```

Where:
- **Net Interest Income (NII)** = Total Interest Income − Total Interest Expense
- **Average Earning Assets** = Average of opening and closing balances of all
  interest-bearing assets for the period
- Result expressed as annualised percentage

---

## 3. Components

### 3.1 Interest Income (Numerator — Positive)
All income earned on interest-bearing assets including:
- Loan interest (retail, corporate, SME, mortgage)
- Investment securities income
- Interbank lending income
- Central bank deposit income
- Fee income that is integral to the effective interest rate (EIR)

### 3.2 Interest Expense (Numerator — Negative)
All costs incurred on interest-bearing liabilities including:
- Deposit interest (CASA, fixed deposits, certificates of deposit)
- Borrowing costs (interbank, wholesale funding)
- Subordinated debt interest
- Lease liabilities interest (IFRS 16)

### 3.3 Average Earning Assets (Denominator)
All assets that generate interest income:
- Gross loans and advances (before provisions)
- Investment securities (held-to-maturity, available-for-sale)
- Interbank placements
- Central bank reserves (interest-bearing portion)
- Finance lease receivables

**Excluded from earning assets:**
- Cash and non-interest-bearing central bank reserves
- Non-performing loans written off
- Goodwill and intangible assets
- Fixed assets and right-of-use assets
- Deferred tax assets

---

## 4. Calculation Period

| Period Type | Calculation Method |
|---|---|
| Daily | Actual NII for day / Average earning assets for day × 365 |
| MTD | Cumulative NII month-to-date / Average earning assets MTD × (365/days elapsed) |
| QTD | Cumulative NII quarter-to-date / Average earning assets QTD × (365/days elapsed) |
| YTD | Cumulative NII year-to-date / Average earning assets YTD × (365/days elapsed) |
| Full Year | Annual NII / Average annual earning assets × 100 |

## 6. Key Drivers of NIM Movement

### NIM Expansion Drivers (positive)
- Increase in lending rates (rate repricing on variable loans)
- Portfolio mix shift toward higher-yielding assets (e.g. SME vs mortgage)
- Reduction in cost of funds (CASA ratio improvement)
- New loan origination at rates above portfolio average
- Reduction in non-performing loans (restoring interest accrual)

### NIM Compression Drivers (negative)
- Central bank rate cuts flowing through to loan rates faster than deposit rates
- Competitive pressure reducing loan spreads
- Mix shift toward lower-yielding assets (e.g. government bonds)
- Increase in wholesale funding costs
- NPL increase causing interest income suspension
- Excess liquidity deployed in low-yield instruments

---

## 7. Segment Decomposition

NIM should be reported and investigated at the following dimensions:
- **Business unit**: Retail, Corporate, Treasury, SME, Wealth
- **Product type**: Mortgage, Auto, Personal Loan, Business Loan, Trade Finance
- **Geography**: By region and country
- **Currency**: Local currency vs foreign currency NIM

## 9. Exclusions and Adjustments

- **Fee income**: Include only fees that are integral to EIR under IFRS 9.
  Standalone fees (e.g. account maintenance) are excluded from NIM and
  reported under Non-Interest Income.
- **Provision charges**: Excluded from NIM calculation. Reported separately
  as credit impairment charges.
- **FX translation**: All foreign currency balances translated at spot rate
  on reporting date. FX gains/losses excluded from NIM.
- **Intercompany**: Intercompany loans and deposits eliminated at
  consolidated level.

---

## 10. Reconciliation Rules

The NIM reported in kpi.net_interest_margin must reconcile within 0.01%
tolerance to:
1. Monthly income statement NII line item
2. Regulatory COREP NII submission
3. ALCO committee NIM report

Any reconciliation break exceeding 0.01% must be investigated and resolved
within 2 business days with root cause documented in audit.query_log.

---

## 11. Related KPIs

- **CASA Ratio**: Higher CASA reduces cost of funds, expanding NIM
- **NPL Ratio**: Higher NPL suspends interest accrual, compressing NIM
- **LCR/NSFR**: Excess liquidity held in HQLA compresses NIM
- **Cost-to-Income**: NIM improvement flows through to CIR improvement
